My Work

Mystidel: ROBLOX MMORPG

Mystidel is a passion project in my freshman year of high school. It is an MMORPG on the Roblox platform consisting of many aspects such as a storyline, bosses and quests. The game currently has around 75,000 lines of code, consisting of something large like the game's main database for stats (HP, Stamina, Mana) and something small like a player's inventory. While Roblox has a physics simulation by default, I implemeted my own form of a physics simulation for some moves like magic projectiles or dashing. As the Project-Lead of Mystidel, I am tasked with making sure the team fulfills tasks and coding the majority of the game. The game was coded completely in rLua. The game is still in development.

Cube-Shaped Server

At the time of creation, I was a college student in my second year, undergoing the process of transferring to another school. I have never stayed in a dorm before, and knew many people would be using the same wifi I was using. As a result, I would be restricted to an extent. The solution to this problem, you ask? Making my own server of course! All jokes aside, I wanted to make a project with a Raspberry Pi 4, and thought that unlike most projects, this one is very useful. It also comes with UPS and SSD storage! At the end of the day, every server in the world is a computer, and so is a Rasperry Pi. The original design of the server rack is created by Michael Klements.

Pomodoro Timer

As a student in college, I undergo tedious amounts of studying. Over my time spent in college, I learned to work smarter not harder. Instead of brute forcing my way through studying, I learned better methods. One of them is the Pomodoro method. In this method, a student takes intervals of studying like 30 minutes with 5-15 minute breaks, instead of non-stop studying with no breaks. I was able to make a Pomodoro Timer that would help me in my studies via java script.
